Core Strategy: Driving Adoption and Utilization

RxSight's strategy is focused on making its technology the standard of care in premium cataract surgery:

  • Expand the Installed Base: Place Light Delivery Device (LDD) systems in high-volume cataract surgery practices to create a captive market for the lenses.
  • Increase LAL Procedures: Once an LDD is placed, work with the practice to drive the utilization of Light Adjustable Lenses per system.
  • Highlight Superior Outcomes: Leverage strong clinical data showing the LAL's ability to deliver superior, customized vision compared to traditional premium lenses.
  • International Expansion: Grow its commercial footprint in key international markets to broaden its addressable market.

How RxSight Makes Money: The Razor and Blades Model

RxSight's business model is designed for long-term, recurring growth. It consists of the initial sale of a capital equipment system (the "razor") and the ongoing, high-margin sale of a proprietary consumable (the "blades").

The Blades: Light Adjustable Lenses (LALs)

This is the primary long-term revenue and profit engine for the company. The LAL is the proprietary, single-use intraocular lens implanted during cataract surgery. Made of photosensitive silicone, its shape and power can be precisely modified post-surgery. Sales of LALs are recurring and grow as the installed base of LDDs and utilization per site increase.

The Razor: Light Delivery Devices (LDDs)

The LDD is the capital equipment that ophthalmologists purchase to be able to offer the LAL procedure. It is an in-office device that uses UV light to perform the non-invasive adjustments to the LAL. Each LDD placement creates a new customer for the recurring LAL revenue stream.
