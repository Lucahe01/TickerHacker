Company Overview

Sempra is a leading North American energy infrastructure company, delivering energy to nearly 40 million consumers. Its operations are structured around three core pillars—Sempra California, Sempra Texas, and Sempra Infrastructure—balancing stable regulated utility operations with high-growth infrastructure development, particularly in Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG).

Sempra's Business Model: Three Pillars

Sempra's business model strategically combines the stability of regulated utilities with the growth potential of large-scale infrastructure projects. This hybrid approach provides predictable cash flows from its California and Texas utilities to support capital-intensive ventures in its Infrastructure segment.

1. Sempra California

Operations: San Diego Gas & Electric (SDG&E) and SoCalGas, serving 25M consumers.

Services: Electric and Natural Gas Transmission & Distribution.

Revenue Model: Regulated rates set by the CPUC, allowing recovery of costs and a return on rate base investment.

2. Sempra Texas

Operations: Majority ownership of Oncor, the largest T&D utility in Texas, serving 13M consumers.

Services: Electric Transmission & Distribution.

Revenue Model: Regulated rates set by the PUCT to cover costs and provide an authorized return on equity.

3. Sempra Infrastructure

Operations: LNG export facilities (Cameron, ECA, Port Arthur), natural gas pipelines, and low-carbon solutions.

Services: LNG production & export, natural gas transportation.

Revenue Model: Long-term contracts for LNG & pipeline capacity, equity earnings from JVs, asset sales.

Competitive Landscape

Sempra's hybrid model differentiates it from pure-play utilities and infrastructure firms. Its regulated utilities provide stable cash flow to de-risk capital-intensive LNG projects, offering a unique value proposition.

Sempra's Differentiated Model vs. Competitors

vs. Utilities (e.g., PG&E, Edison Int'l)

Unlike pure-play utilities confined to regulated rate base growth, Sempra's Infrastructure arm provides a significant vector for higher growth through its large-scale LNG projects. Sempra also has greater geographic diversification (CA, TX, Mexico) compared to state-focused peers.

vs. Infrastructure (e.g., Cheniere, Kinder Morgan)

Compared to pure-play LNG or midstream companies, Sempra's extensive and stable regulated utility base offers a distinct advantage. This foundation provides predictable earnings to support and de-risk the more volatile and capital-intensive nature of its infrastructure projects.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Diversified portfolio (regulated & infrastructure)
  • Large, stable regulated rate base
  • Strategic asset locations (CA, TX, LNG hubs)
  • Significant growth pipeline ($56B capex)

Weaknesses

  • High regulatory exposure (esp. California)
  • Heavy capital expenditure requirements

  • Interest rate and commodity price sensitivity

Threats

Concluding Thoughts

Sempra is positioned at the nexus of key energy trends: grid modernization, electrification, and global LNG demand. Its success hinges on executing its massive capital plan, navigating complex regulations, and managing project risks. The hybrid model offers a compelling profile, balancing stability with high-growth potential in the evolving energy landscape.
