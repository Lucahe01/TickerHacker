Dexcom: Leading the CGM Revolution in Diabetes Care

Dexcom is a leading medical device company focused on the design and commercialization of continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) systems. Its technology has transformed diabetes management by replacing painful, episodic fingersticks with a continuous, real-time stream of glucose data. The company's strategy is centered on relentless innovation, exemplified by the transition to its new G7 platform, and expanding access to new patient populations. As CGM shifts from a niche tool to the standard of care, Dexcom is poised to capitalize on a massive and underpenetrated global market.

Core Strategy: Innovation and Market Expansion

Dexcom's growth strategy is built on several key pillars:

  • Technology Leadership: Driving the adoption of the G7 CGM system, which is 60% smaller than the G6 and has a much faster 30-minute sensor warm-up time.
  • Expanding Access: Securing broader reimbursement coverage to expand into the vast Type 2 diabetes market, including patients on basal-only insulin therapy.
  • Automated Insulin Delivery (AID): Deeply integrating its CGM with insulin pump partners like Tandem and Insulet to create "artificial pancreas" systems.
  • Global Growth: Increasing penetration in international markets where CGM adoption is still in its early stages.

How Dexcom Makes Money: A Recurring Revenue Model

Dexcom's business model is primarily based on the recurring sale of its single-use, wearable CGM sensors, which creates a highly predictable and durable revenue stream. The company reports its revenue based on geography.

U.S. Revenue

The United States is Dexcom's largest and most established market. Growth is driven by the increasing adoption of CGM technology, the successful launch of the G7 system, and the recent expansion of Medicare coverage to include basal insulin users, which significantly grew the addressable market.
